Two Winter Gardens, two kinds of roof work

Horizon West is one of Central Florida's heaviest concentrations of tile roofs — village after village of newer construction with concrete tile, active HOAs, and exacting community standards. Tile here shows mildew shadowing and algae film before it shows shingle-style streaks, and the cleaning method is built around the material: low pressure, minimal walking, edge application. Because the communities are young, many owners are hitting their first-ever roof cleaning now, as the original construction shine wears into the first visible growth.

Old Winter Garden is the opposite job: established shingle roofs around the historic downtown, Plant Street, and the lakefront, many under mature oaks that keep slopes damp and feed moss in the valleys. These roofs run the classic canopy cycle — faster regrowth, more moss and lichen, more care required on older shingle surfaces with no granules to spare.
