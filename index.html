What roof cleaning fixes

The classic Central Florida complaint is black streaks running down shingle slopes — that is roof algae, and it is the most common call. Close behind: green growth and moss on shaded slopes, lichen crusts on older shingles, mildew shadows on tile, and the general gray dinge that makes a fifteen-year-old roof look thirty. All of it is biological growth, which means all of it comes off with the right chemistry — and none of it comes off safely with raw pressure. That is the whole argument for soft washing, the low-pressure method used here.

Shingle and tile are different jobs

Most Orlando roofs are asphalt shingle, where the risk is granule loss — the gravel-like surface layer that does the actual weather protection. Pressure washing blasts granules off; soft washing leaves them alone. Tile roofs — common in Winter Garden’s Horizon West, Lake Mary’s gated communities, and newer construction across the metro — bring a different problem: tile cracks underfoot, so the cleaning plan has to manage where anyone steps as much as what gets sprayed. Knowing your roof material before you call helps, but the callback conversation sorts it out either way.

The HOA letter problem

A large share of roof cleaning in this market starts with an envelope: the HOA noticed the streaks before you did, and now there is a deadline. The HOA notice page walks through how that process usually goes — what the letters actually require, how much time is realistic, and what documentation closes the matter with the association. Short version: these letters are routine, the fix is straightforward, and the deadline is almost always workable if you do not sit on it.

Why Orlando roofs streak so fast

Heat, humidity, and afternoon rain make Central Florida close to ideal for roof algae, and the spores travel on the wind — which is why streaks march down a street one roof at a time. Oak canopy makes it worse twice over: shade keeps slopes damp longer, and debris feeds growth in the valleys. North-facing slopes streak first, shaded slopes streak worst, and a cleaned roof stays clean longest where it gets sun. None of this is a defect in your roof. It is the climate, and it is manageable on a cycle of every few years.

Frequently asked questions

What are the black streaks on my roof?

Almost always roof algae — Gloeocapsa magma, a bacteria that feeds on the limestone filler in asphalt shingles and thrives in Central Florida humidity. The streaks are living growth, not permanent stains, which is why a proper soft wash removes them rather than just fading them.

Is soft washing safe for my roof?

Yes — it is the method shingle manufacturers point to. Soft washing uses low pressure with a cleaning solution that kills the algae at the root. Pressure washing is the unsafe option: it strips shingle granules, can crack tiles, and can void roof warranties.

How long do the results last?

Typically a few years in Central Florida — shaded, tree-covered roofs regrow faster than open, sunny ones. Because soft washing kills the organism instead of rinsing the surface, results last far longer than pressure washing the streaks off.

I got an HOA letter about my roof. Can you work with the deadline?

Yes — HOA roof-cleaning notices are one of the most common reasons people call. Mention the deadline when you reach out and see the HOA notice page for how the process usually runs, including documentation for the association.

Will the cleaning hurt my plants or lawn?

Landscaping protection is part of the job, not an afterthought: plants and grass under the roofline get pre-wet, covered where needed, and rinsed after. Ask any company you hire how they handle runoff — it is a fair test of whether they do this work properly.
