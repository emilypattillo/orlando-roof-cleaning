What grows on tile

Tile shows its age differently than shingle. Instead of black streaks, the usual complaints are green-black mildew shadowing on shaded slopes, algae film that dulls the tile color, dark grime lines below ridge caps and in the channels, and white efflorescence blooms on concrete tile — that last one is mineral salts, not growth, and it weathers differently than algae does. The biological growth all responds to the same low-pressure chemistry used on shingle; the cleaning plan just changes around the material.

The walking problem

The defining constraint of tile work: tiles crack under concentrated foot traffic, and a cracked tile is a water path to the underlayment. Proper tile cleaning minimizes time on the roof, distributes weight along the strong points of each tile when walking is unavoidable, and applies the solution from edges, ridges, and lifts wherever the geometry allows. A company that plans to stroll across your barrel tile with a pressure washer is offering to create the leak they will later quote you to find.

Why pressure is doubly wrong on tile

Beyond cracking risk, pressure washing drives water sideways under the tile overlaps and into the underlayment — the waterproof layer that does the real work on a tile roof. Older underlayment is exactly what you do not want to soak. Soft washing keeps water behaving like rain, which the roof was built for.

About sealing

After cleaning, some concrete-tile owners consider sealing to slow regrowth and refresh color. It is a real option with real tradeoffs — cost, periodic reapplication, and surface changes — and it is a separate decision from cleaning, not a bundled upsell. If you are curious, raise it during the quote conversation and weigh it on its own merits.

Frequently asked questions

Is it safe to walk on my tile roof?

For you, no — tiles crack under concentrated weight and falls from tile are unforgiving. Professional tile work minimizes walking entirely and distributes weight correctly when it is unavoidable.

What are the white blotches on my concrete tile?

Usually efflorescence — mineral salts migrating out of the concrete — rather than growth. It is cosmetic, behaves differently from algae, and is worth identifying correctly before anyone quotes a cleaning for it.

Will cleaning restore the tile color?

It removes the growth and grime dulling the surface, which usually recovers most of the color. Pigment faded by decades of UV does not come back with cleaning — that is where the sealing conversation starts.

Do broken tiles get replaced during cleaning?

Cleaning and tile repair are separate scopes, but cracked or slipped tiles spotted during the work should be flagged to you — finding them early is one of the side benefits of getting eyes on the roof.
